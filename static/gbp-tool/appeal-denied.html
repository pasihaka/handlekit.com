Appeal denied guide

What to review before resubmitting evidence

An appeal denial does not mean the exact issue is obvious. It usually means the next move should be stronger evidence and a cleaner consistency review.

This tool gives preparation guidance only. It does not diagnose the exact reason for suspension, replace official Google guidance, provide legal advice, or guarantee reinstatement.

What to review before you send anything else

Business eligibility consistency

Make sure the chosen business model, public address setup, and real-world operations all match each other.

Evidence quality

Look for mismatched names, stale photos, incomplete documents, weak location proof, or missing service-area evidence.

Ownership proof

Review whether you can clearly prove control of the business and the relationship to the location.

Path correctness

Make sure you are using the correct official route for the actual status instead of a generic re-appeal loop.

What not to do

  • Do not resubmit blindly without tightening the evidence set first.
  • Do not send documents that conflict with the profile name, address, phone, or business type.
  • Do not keep editing core profile fields while you are still trying to understand the evidence gap.
  • Do not treat a virtual office or mailbox like a staffed storefront.

Evidence-prep checklist

  • Current business registration or license.
  • Current utility bill, lease, or other location relationship proof if the profile uses a public address.
  • Fresh photos that match the location, signage, tools, vehicles, and real operations.
  • Website and phone details that match what appears in the profile and evidence.
  • Service-area proof if the business serves customers away from the listed location.
