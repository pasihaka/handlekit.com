Disabled profile guide

Disabled is not the same as suspended

If the profile is disabled, the first review often needs to focus harder on ownership, business legitimacy, and account control before appeal.

This tool gives preparation guidance only. It does not diagnose the exact reason for suspension, replace official Google guidance, provide legal advice, or guarantee reinstatement.

Suspended vs disabled

Suspended

Often points to eligibility, representation, or evidence problems that need to be corrected before appeal.

Disabled

Often requires a stricter review of ownership, access, and legitimacy, not just a quick location or signage check.

What to check first

  • Who controls the profile and whether the current manager or owner relationship is documented.
  • Whether the business name, address, phone, and website all match across the listing and public web presence.
  • Whether the business can prove a real relationship to the listed location if a public address is used.
  • Whether the chosen business type actually matches the operating model.

Appeal implications

Disabled status is a place where blind resubmission is especially risky. The next step usually needs cleaner ownership proof and a more consistent evidence set.

A disabled profile does not automatically mean the same evidence package used for a suspension will be enough.
