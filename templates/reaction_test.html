The Science of Neural Latency

Human reaction time is the temporal bridge between sensory input and motor output. This gap, measured in milliseconds (ms), is known as neural latency. In a classic visual reaction test, light strikes the retina, travels through the optic nerve to the primary visual cortex, is processed in the prefrontal cortex, and finally triggers a signal to the motor cortex to initiate a physical movement.

T(total) = T(sensory) + T(cognitive) + T(motor)

While the average human reaction time is approximately 250ms for visual stimuli, elite performers such as Formula 1 drivers or professional gamers often reach latencies beneath 200ms. Factors affecting these results include neuroplasticity, physical fatigue, and even the refresh rate of your display hardware.
