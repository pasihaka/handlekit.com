Color Format Guide

  • HEX, #RRGGBB, the web standard for CSS
  • RGB, used for screens and digital light
  • HSL, intuitive control over saturation/lightness
  • CMYK, the professional choice for print

Palette Types Explained

  • Complementary, opposite hues, high contrast
  • Analogous, neighbouring hues, harmonious
  • Triadic, three equally-spaced hues, vibrant
  • Shades, light to dark variants of your colour

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pick a color and get its values?

Use our responsive Color Wheel to dial in a specific hue, or use the Pipette icon (EyeDropper) to sample any pixel directly from your display. The values for HEX, RGB, HSL, and CMYK will sync in real-time. Pro tip: you can also type a specific HEX code into the input field to jump directly to a known color. Use the copy icons to grab any format instantly.

Which color format should I use in my code?

HEX is the industry standard for CSS—it's concise and reliable. However, many modern developers prefer HSL because it makes adjusting lightness or saturation intuitive. Use RGB if you need to add transparency (opacity) to your elements. CMYK is reserved specifically for physical print projects.

How do I generate a color palette?

Pick your base color using the wheel or EyeDropper. Below the value display, the Generated Palette shows four expert modes: Complementary, Analogous, Triadic, and Shades. Switch between them to see different harmonious combinations. Click any swatch in the palette to instantly copy its HEX value to your clipboard with a visual confirmation.

What's the difference between complementary and analogous?

Complementary colors are high-contrast opposites on the wheel, perfect for making elements (like buttons) stand out. Analogous colors sit side-by-side, creating a smooth, harmonious look that feels natural and professional. Use complementary for "pop" and analogous for "blend."

Does the Color Picker or EyeDropper store my data?

No data is stored or transmitted. The EyeDropper tool uses your browser's native capabilities to pick pixels, and all color calculations happen entirely in your local session. Nothing is sent to our servers. If you close the tab, your picks are gone, so copy the values you need before navigating away.
