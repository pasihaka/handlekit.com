What Is a Color Picker?

A color picker lets you select any color and retrieve its values in every common format: HEX (used in HTML/CSS), RGB (screen displays and design tools), HSL (intuitive for developers), and CMYK (standard for print). HandleKit's Color Picker also generates four palette types to help you build harmonious colour schemes instantly, all in your browser, with zero data leaving your device.

Colour Format Guide

  • HEX, #RRGGBB, used in CSS & HTML
  • RGB, rgb(R, G, B), values 0–255
  • HSL, hsl(H°, S%, L%), great for devs
  • CMYK, for print design & publishing

Palette Types Explained

  • Complementary, opposite hues, high contrast
  • Analogous, neighbouring hues, harmonious
  • Triadic, three equally-spaced hues, vibrant
  • Shades, light to dark variants of your colour

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pick a colour and get its values?

Click the colour wheel on the left to open your browser's native colour picker. As you select a colour, the HEX, RGB, HSL, and CMYK values update instantly. You can also type a HEX code directly into the HEX field and press Enter to jump straight to that colour. Click any copy button to copy that specific format to your clipboard.

What colour format should I use in CSS?

HEX (#RRGGBB) is the most common for simple colours, clean, concise, and universally supported. RGB is useful when you need to manipulate individual channels in code or use transparency (rgba()). HSL is increasingly popular for theming because it's intuitive: you can bump the lightness or saturation value and know exactly what will change. CMYK is for print design, not web.

How do I generate a colour palette?

Pick your base colour using the colour wheel. Below the value display, you'll find the Generated Palette section with four mode buttons: Complementary, Analogous, Triadic, and Shades. Switch between them to see different harmonious palette options based on colour theory. Click any swatch in the palette to make it your new base colour.

What's the difference between complementary and analogous palettes?

Complementary colours sit opposite each other on the colour wheel, they create high-contrast combinations that pop. Think blue and orange. Analogous colours sit next to each other, giving a more harmonious, cohesive feel, like a green-teal-blue gradient. Complementary for emphasis, analogous for elegance.

Does the colour picker store my picks?

No data is stored anywhere. All colour calculations happen entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server. If you close the tab, your picks are gone, so copy the values you need before navigating away.
