Why QR Codes are Essential for Modern Business

QR (Quick Response) codes have transitioned from a niche technology to a universal bridge between the physical and digital worlds. Whether you are a business owner placing a menu on a table or a creator sharing your portfolio at a networking event, QR codes provide a frictionless way for users to access your content instantly.

Our generator creates Static QR Codes, meaning the information is encoded directly into the pattern. This makes them permanent—they will never expire, and they don't rely on a middle-man service to function.

High-Resolution & Custom Sizes

Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes

Static QR Codes (like the ones we generate) embed the target data directly in the code itself. They are best for permanent links like website URLs or contact information. Dynamic QR Codes use a redirect service, allowing you to change the destination later. While dynamic codes are useful for tracking, static codes are more reliable, faster, and more private.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create a QR code?

Enter your URL or any text into the input box on the left, the tool starts with a preview automatically. Choose your preferred size (Small for digital use, Medium for most purposes, Large or Custom for print), select an error correction level, and click "Generate QR Code". Once the QR code appears in the preview, the "Download PNG" button activates and you can save the image at your selected resolution.

What size QR code do I need?

For on-screen use and social media, 400×400 pixels is plenty. For business cards, aim for at least 600px. For A4 printed flyers, use 1000–1200px. For large-format printing (banners, posters, signage), go custom with 2000–3000px. The minimum scannable size in print is roughly 2×2 cm, smaller than that and some phone cameras struggle.

What does "error correction level" mean?

QR codes have built-in redundancy that lets them be read even when partially damaged or covered. Low (L) can recover from up to 7% damage, Medium (M) handles 15%, Quartile (Q) handles 25%, and High (H) handles 30%. Higher correction makes the QR slightly more complex (denser), but it's a good choice for physical print where the code might get scratched or dirty.

Do these QR codes expire?

No, never. HandleKit generates static QR codes, meaning the destination URL is encoded directly into the QR pattern itself. There's no middleware service involved, so there's nothing that can expire, go offline, or disappear. The QR code you download today will still work in ten years, as long as the URL it points to still exists.

Can I encode something other than a URL?

Yes, absolutely. You can encode any plain text, a phone number, an email address, a Wi-Fi password (formatted as WIFI:T:WPA;S:YourNetwork;P:YourPassword;;), a physical address, or even a short poem. QR codes don't care what the content is; they just encode whatever string you provide.
