Keyboard Walks

Patterns like `qwerty`, `asdfgh`, or `123456` are known as "keyboard walks". Hackers don't even need dictionary lists to crack these; their algorithms automatically try all adjacent keyboard key combinations first.

Personal Context

Using your pet's name, your birth year, or your favorite sports team (`yankees2024`) is extremely dangerous. Targeted attacks scrape your social media profiles to build custom dictionaries explicitly for you.

Suffix Padding

Taking a weak password and adding `!` or `123` to the end (`password123!`) does not make it strong. Cracking rigs automatically append common symbols and numbers to every dictionary word they try.
