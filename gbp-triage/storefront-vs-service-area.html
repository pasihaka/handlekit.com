Storefront vs. Service-Area Business

Why this classification dictates your entire evidence strategy.

Important: This is preparation guidance, not a diagnosis or a guarantee of reinstatement.

Why This Matters

Classification dictates your evidence strategy. Storefronts are generally scrutinized for permanent signs and staffed desks during verification. Service-Area Businesses (SABs) are evaluated on legitimate local presence, official vehicles, and service tracking rather than visible signage.

What Evidence is Useful in Each Case

  • Storefronts: Physical location photos, external shots of the building from the street, explicit signage.
  • Service-Area Businesses: Your registered address is usually expected to be a legitimate residential home or commercial base, but is commonly required to be hidden on Maps. Evidence heavily relies on branded trucks, state licenses, and localized invoices.
